
# **ACTL6B-Related Syndrome**

### Disease Overview

ACTL6B-related syndrome—also known as developmental and epileptic encephalopathy-76 or intellectual developmental disorder with severe speech and ambulation defects (IDDSSAD)—is a genetic condition caused by variants in the ACTL6B gene. This gene produces a protein essential for the transport of cellular components and for regulating gene expression by turning DNA off. The syndrome occurs when genetic changes prevent this gene from functioning correctly, affecting brain development and activity.

### Epidemiology/Prevalence

The condition is considered rare, with at least 105 individuals described in medical research as of 2024.

### Clinical Features

The clinical presentation varies depending on whether the condition is autosomal dominant (one gene variant) or autosomal recessive (two gene variants).

### Diagnosis

The syndrome is diagnosed through genetic testing that identifies damaging variants in the ACTL6B gene. A genetic diagnosis is considered essential for tracking the condition and managing appropriate therapies.

### Natural History
While the long-term progression is still being studied, research indicates that approximately 22% of individuals with the autosomal recessive form experience a regression of motor and cognitive abilities.
