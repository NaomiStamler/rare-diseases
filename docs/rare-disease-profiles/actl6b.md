
### ACTL6B
### **ACTL6B Disease Profile**
Disease Overview ACTL6B-related syndrome—also known as developmental and epileptic encephalopathy-76 or intellectual developmental disorder with severe speech and ambulation defects (IDDSSAD)—is a genetic condition caused by variants in the ACTL6B gene1. This gene produces a protein essential for the transport of cellular components and for regulating gene expression by turning DNA off1. The syndrome occurs when genetic changes prevent this gene from functioning correctly, affecting brain development and activity1,2.
Epidemiology/PrevalenceThe condition is considered rare, with at least 105 individuals described in medical research as of 20243.
Clinical FeaturesThe clinical presentation varies depending on whether the condition is autosomal dominant (one gene variant) or autosomal recessive (two gene variants)4.
How It’s DiagnosedThe syndrome is diagnosed through genetic testing that identifies damaging variants in the ACTL6B gene3. A genetic diagnosis is considered essential for tracking the condition and managing appropriate therapies3.
Natural HistoryWhile the long-term progression is still being studied, research indicates that approximately 22% of individuals with the autosomal recessive form experience a regression of motor and cognitive abilities10,11.
