### Disease Profile: Hereditary Spastic Paraplegia 35 (SPG35)Disease Overview
Hereditary Spastic Paraplegia 35 (SPG35) is an autosomal recessive form of hereditary spastic paraplegia (HSP) caused by mutations in the fatty acid 2-hydroxylase (FA2H) gene, located on chromosome 16q23.11.2. The FA2H gene produces a protein involved in incorporating ceramide groups for myelin production2. Mutations in this gene lead to neurodegenerative processes specifically associated with higher iron residues in the brain2.
General HSP Context: HSPs are a large group of monogenic neurological disorders characterized by axonal atrophy in the corticospinal tract and dorsal spinal cord3. They are clinically divided into "pure" forms (limited to lower-limb spasticity and weakness) and "complex" forms (associated with additional neurological findings)3.4.Epidemiology/Prevalence
Specific prevalence rates for SPG35 are not addressed in available sources. However, SPG35 is cited as one of the prevalent forms of autosomal recessive HSP, a category that is more common in populations with high rates of consanguineous marriage5.6.
