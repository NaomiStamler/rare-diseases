### Coats Plus Syndrome Disease Profile
Disease OverviewCoats plus syndrome is a rare, pleiotropic multisystem condition characterized by a specific eye disorder known as Coats disease, alongside significant abnormalities affecting the brain, bones, gastrointestinal system, and other organs1.2. It is also known by the synonym cerebroretinal microangiopathy with calcifications and cysts (CRMCC)3.4. The condition is caused by genetic mutations and follows an autosomal recessive inheritance pattern, meaning an affected individual must inherit two copies of the mutated gene, one from each parent5.6.
Epidemiology/PrevalenceThe prevalence of Coats plus syndrome is estimated to be less than 1 in 1,000,0004.

Natural HistorySymptoms typically begin to appear during childhood4,12. The brain abnormalities associated with the syndrome are progressive, worsening over time and leading to increasing physical and intellectual impairment1. Eye complications can likewise progress from mild vessel abnormalities to total retinal detachment1,7.
Therapeutic LandscapeBecause only approximately 5% of rare diseases have FDA-approved treatments, management focus is often on symptom control and multidisciplinary care13,14.
