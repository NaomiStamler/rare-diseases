
### MYT1L Syndrome
### **MYT1L Syndrome Disease Profile****Disease Overview**
MYT1L-related developmental delay-intellectual disability-obesity syndrome is a rare neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by a range of cognitive, behavioral, and physical symptoms1. It is also known as MYT1L-associated neurodevelopmental disorder2. The condition is caused by a loss of function of the MYT1L gene, which can result from point mutations or chromosome 2p25.3 deletions3. The MYT1L protein serves as a myelin transcription factor3.**Epidemiology/Prevalence**
The exact prevalence of this syndrome is currently unknown2. To date, more than 100 cases have been reported in medical literature2.**Clinical Features**

Diagnosis is confirmed through cytogenetic or molecular genetic testing due to the nonspecific nature of the clinical symptoms3. It may be identified via exome or genome sequencing, including during pregnancy screening for malformative syndromes4.**Natural History**
The syndrome manifests during the neonatal period or early childhood2. Ambulation (walking) is typically acquired at a median age of 22 months2. Difficulties are often first identified in kindergarten, and most children require adapted schooling2. While the general prognosis is considered good, long-term data on adult patients is currently lacking5.
