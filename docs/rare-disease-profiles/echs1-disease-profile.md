### ECHS1 Deficiency Disease Profile
Disease Overview ECHS1 (short-chain enoyl-CoA hydratase 1) deficiency is a rare mitochondrial disorder caused by mutations in the ECHS1 gene located on chromosome 10q26.31.2. This gene encodes a mitochondrial matrix enzyme essential for fatty acid β-oxidation and branched-chain amino acid (BCAA) metabolism, specifically the degradation of valine1−3. Mutations typically lead to reduced or absent enzyme activity, causing mitochondrial dysfunction, energy failure in high-demand tissues, and the accumulation of toxic metabolic intermediates2,4,5.
Epidemiology/PrevalenceThe exact prevalence is not addressed in available sources, though it is characterized broadly as a rare metabolic and neurodegenerative disorder2.
