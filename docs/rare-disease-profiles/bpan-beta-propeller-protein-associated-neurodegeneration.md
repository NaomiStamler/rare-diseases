### BPAN (Beta-propeller protein-associated neurodegeneration) Disease Profile**Disease Overview**
Beta-propeller protein-associated neurodegeneration (BPAN) is an extremely rare, progressive neurodegenerative disorder1.2. It is classified as a type of Neurodegeneration with Brain Iron Accumulation (NBIA), a group of diseases characterized by excessive iron deposits in deep brain regions, particularly the basal ganglia (such as the globus pallidus and substantia nigra)1−3.
The disease is caused by mutations in the WDR45 gene located on the X chromosome4.5. This gene provides instructions for the WIPI4 protein, which is essential for autophagy—the cellular process of clearing waste and damaged components3,6. Mutations lead to a loss of function in WIPI4, causing toxic materials to accumulate in brain cells, eventually resulting in neurodegeneration3,4,6.**Epidemiology and Prevalence****Clinical Features**
