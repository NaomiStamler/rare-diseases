
### CTNNB1
### **CTNNB1 Syndrome Disease Profile****Disease Overview**
CTNNB1 Syndrome is a severe neurodevelopmental disorder caused by a disruption or mutation of the CTNNB1 gene located on chromosome 3p22.11.2. This gene is responsible for encoding beta-catenin, a protein that plays a critical role in cell-to-cell adhesion and communication, as well as the maturation and development of the brain2−4. Disruption of this gene leads to learning and memory impairments and is primarily associated with developmental and intellectual disabilities2,5.**Epidemiology/Prevalence**
The condition is estimated to affect 1 out of 50,000 children worldwide2.4. It is frequently cited as the most common cause of misdiagnosed cerebral palsy cases1.3. While the syndrome is inherited in an autosomal dominant pattern, most cases occur de novo, meaning the mutation happened spontaneously and was not inherited from the parentsBull 2.3.**Clinical Features**
