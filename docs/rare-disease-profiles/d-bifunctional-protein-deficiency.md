
### D-Bifunctional Protein Deficiency
### **D-Bifunctional Protein Deficiency (D-BPD)****Disease Overview**
D-bifunctional protein deficiency (D-BPD) is a rare, autosomal recessive neurodegenerative disorder caused by mutations in the HSD17B4 gene1.2. This gene encodes the D-bifunctional protein (D-BP), a steroid-metabolizing enzyme involved in the peroxisomal beta-oxidation of very-long-chain fatty acids, branched-chain fatty acids, and the synthesis of bile acids3.4. D-BPD is characterized by a progressive and typically lethal clinical course1,2. Most affected individuals experience severe neurodegeneration and die within the first two years of life1,5,9.
