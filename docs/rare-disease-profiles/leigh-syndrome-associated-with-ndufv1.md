### Disease Profile: Leigh Syndrome Associated with NDUFV1
Disease OverviewLeigh syndrome, also known as subacute necrotizing encephalomyelopathy, is a mitochondrial complex-I-related disorder and is considered one of the most common mitochondrial cytopathies1. It is primarily caused by pathogenic variants in the NDUFV1 gene, which resides in the nuclear DNA and encodes a vital 51-kDa protein subunit of the mitochondrial complex-I1−3. This protein is responsible for oxidizing NADH and producing reactive oxygen species as part of the oxidative-phosphorylation system1. Variants in this gene disrupt the electron input module, leading to significant mitochondrial dysfunctionBull 2.3.
Epidemiology/PrevalenceThe sources characterize NDUFV1-related disorders as a rare entity4. While complex-I deficiency is pan-ethnic, approximately 50% of reported cases in synthesized cohorts are of Asian ethnicity5.6. The c.1156C>T (p.Arg386Cys) variant is identified as a prevalent founder variant among the Asian population4,7.
