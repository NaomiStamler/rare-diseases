
### Surfactant Protein B Deficiency
### Surfactant Protein B (SP-B) Deficiency Disease Profile**Disease Overview**
Surfactant protein B (SP-B) deficiency is a rare inherited disorder that causes interstitial lung disease1. It is characterized by the body's inability to produce functional surfactant—a mixture of fats and proteins that reduces surface tension in the lungs to prevent alveolar collapse during exhalation1.2. The condition is caused by variants in the SFTPB gene and is inherited in an autosomal recessive pattern, meaning an affected child must inherit a disease-causing gene variant from each parent3.4. Synonyms for the condition include SP-B deficiency, pulmonary surfactant metabolism dysfunction-1 (SMDP1), and congenital pulmonary alveolar proteinosis 15.6.**Epidemiology/Prevalence**
In the United States, the estimated incidence of surfactant B protein deficiency is 1 in 1 million live births7.**Clinical Features**
