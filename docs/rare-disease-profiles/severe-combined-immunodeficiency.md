### Disease Profile: Severe Combined Immunodeficiency (SCID)**Disease Overview**
Severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID) is a rare and life-threatening genetic disorder that severely impairs the immune system's ability to defend the body against infections1. Often referred to as "bubble boy" disease, it results from genetic mutations—of which over 20 are known—that disrupt the development and function of immune cells, primarily T cells and B cells1.2. Because the immune system is virtually non-existent, infants with SCID are highly susceptible to recurrent, severe infections from viruses, bacteria, and fungi1. It is considered a pediatric emergency because it is fatal without prompt treatment1.**Epidemiology/Prevalence****Clinical Features**
