
### Sandhoff Disease
### Sandhoff Disease Profile**Disease Overview**
Sandhoff disease, also known as Type II GM2 Gangliosidosis, is a rare, inherited lysosomal storage disorder caused by mutations in the HEXB gene1.2. These mutations result in a deficiency of the enzyme beta-hexosaminidase, which is necessary to break down certain fats (lipids) called GM2 gangliosidesBull 2.3. Without this enzyme, toxic levels of these fats accumulate in the nerve cells of the brain and spinal cord, leading to progressive neurodegeneration4.5.**Epidemiology and Prevalence****Clinical Features**
