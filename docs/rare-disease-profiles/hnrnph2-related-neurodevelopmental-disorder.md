
### HNRNPH2-Related Neurodevelopmental Disorder
### **Disease Profile: HNRNPH2-Related Neurodevelopmental Disorder****Disease Overview**
HNRNPH2-related neurodevelopmental disorder (HNRNPH2-NDD), also known as Bain Type Syndromic Intellectual Disability, is an X-linked condition characterized by significant developmental challenges that typically manifest before age 12 months1.2. It is primarily caused by de novo pathogenic variants in theHNRNPH2gene, although rare cases of vertical transmission from mothers with skewed X-chromosome inactivation have been reported3.4. The disorder affects both females and males, though it was initially thought to be lethal in males5.**Epidemiology/Prevalence**
The exact prevalence of HNRNPH2-NDD is currently unknown6. As of the available source material, 49 individuals from 45 families have been identified with pathogenic variants inHNRNPH25.6. While it was once hypothesized that the condition was embryonic lethal for males, at least 16 affected males have since been reported5.**Clinical Features**
The clinical presentation of HNRNPH2-NDD involves several major and minor features:**How It’s Diagnosed**
The diagnosis is established through molecular genetic testing identifying a heterozygous or hemizygous pathogenic variant inHNRNPH2in an individual with suggestive clinical findings15,16.**Natural History**
Symptoms typically begin in early infancy (before age 12 months)2,5. While data on the long-term progression is limited, the clinical course does not appear to be progressive or degenerative into early adulthood20. Survival into adulthood is possible, with at least one individual reported alive at age 3820. Most individuals require significant support for activities of daily living throughout their lives9,21.**Therapeutic Landscape**
There are no approved targeted therapies specifically for HNRNPH2-NDD at this time22. Management focuses on treating specific manifestations:**Research (Preclinical, Clinical)**
