
### SPG88
### **Hereditary Spastic Paraplegia 88 (SPG88) Disease Profile****Disease Overview**
Hereditary spastic paraplegias (HSPs) represent a diverse group of monogenic neurological disorders characterized by the degeneration of the corticospinal and dorsal spinal cord axons1. This degeneration typically results in bilateral lower limb spasticity and overactive reflexes1.
SPG88 is a specific autosomal dominant form of HSP caused by a heterozygous mutation in the KPNA3 gene located on chromosome 13q14.2Bull 2.3. While many forms of HSP manifest later in life, SPG88 is distinctively characterized by an onset of symptoms within the first year of life4.**Epidemiology and Prevalence****Clinical Features****How it is Diagnosed****Natural History****Therapeutic Landscape****Research (Preclinical and Clinical)**
