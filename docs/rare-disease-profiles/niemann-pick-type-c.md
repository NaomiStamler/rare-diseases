
### Niemann-Pick Type C
### **Disease Profile: Niemann-Pick Type C (NPC)****Disease Overview**
Niemann-Pick disease, type C (NPC) is a rare, autosomal recessive neurodegenerative and neurovisceral storage disorder1.2. It is primarily caused by mutations in the NPC1 gene (90%-95% of cases) or the NPC2 gene (~5% of cases), which lead to the deficient function of proteins responsible for binding and transporting cholesterol2. This deficiency results in impaired intracellular trafficking, causing unesterified cholesterol and other lipids to accumulate within lysosomes in the brain, liver, spleen, and other tissues2−4. This lysosomal accumulation is the cellular hallmark of the disease3.**Epidemiology/Prevalence**
The incidence of NPC is estimated to be approximately 1 in 100,000 individuals5. However, it is believed that cases with older onset are likely underestimated5.**Clinical Features**
NPC is a highly heterogeneous disease with a clinical presentation that varies significantly depending on the age of onset1.5.**Diagnosis**
Diagnosis of NPC is achieved through several modalities:**Natural History**
NPC is a relentlessly progressive disease that leads to death1. The progression and lifespan vary by onset:**Therapeutic Landscape**
Currently, there is no cure for NPC, and no disease-modifying treatments are approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)23,24.**Research****Preclinical Research**
